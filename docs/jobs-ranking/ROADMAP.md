# @jsonresume/jobs — Product & Tech Roadmap

**Date:** 2026-07-22 · **Scope:** CLI/TUI (Ink) + web job board over the HN "Who is Hiring" corpus (~300-800 jobs/month, nightly refresh) · **Stack:** Next.js/Vercel, Supabase pgvector (`match_jobs_v5` / `embedding_v5`), OpenAI embeddings, gpt-4.1-mini via Vercel AI SDK.

**Baseline assumption:** the eleven fixes from the ranking eval (`/tmp/jobeval/report.md`) land first — wide retrieval pool, 0.95/0.05 decay, location-aware batched reranker, salary ingest, word-boundary keyword, prompt-first HyDE, embedding cache, auth on POST. This roadmap is what comes *after* the floor is fixed.

**The one scale fact that drives everything:** this corpus is hundreds of jobs, not millions. Recall is nearly free, an LLM can read every job every night for under a dollar, and the whole month fits in the TUI's memory. The winning moves are ingest-time enrichment, listwise judging, and honest presentation — not big-IR machinery.

## 1. North-star UX narrative — a day in the life

It's August 1. The HN thread dropped overnight, and by the time Thomas (senior AI/TS engineer, Melbourne, remote-only) opens a terminal, the nightly pipeline has already read all 612 posts with a strict-schema extractor, collapsed 87 reposts into canonical jobs with "still hiring, 3rd month running" badges, and stamped every row with structured facets: remote scope, timezone range, annualized salary with an `(stated)` / `(inferred)` / `(not stated)` provenance tag, seniority, stack, visa.

He types `jobs`. The header reads: **"August thread: 612 posts → 498 unique jobs. 41 pass your hard facets (remote-global or AEST-overlap, $150k+, senior). 9 new since last visit — 3 Strong, 4 Good, 2 Stretch."** No raw cosine numbers anywhere; tiers come from a single listwise LLM pass over the facet-gated pool, and every row carries skill chips — green for `TypeScript · Vercel AI SDK · founding experience`, amber for `voice AI (gap)` — each chip citing actual text from his resume.json or the posting.

The preview pane on the right shows the full post plus a three-bullet grounded pitch: "You fit: they want Vercel AI SDK in production — you maintain jsonresume.org's AI pipeline. They want 0→1 ownership — you were CTO at X. Gap: they'd like shipped voice agents; your closest evidence is the agents work in Y. Angle: lead with the open-source community reach." Salary sits at the top of the card, not buried: `$160-240k (stated)`.

He hits `t` for triage mode and `y`/`n`s through the 9 new jobs in ninety seconds. Two passes on crypto roles apply a bounded, decaying downrank to that neighborhood — the list visibly reorders, nothing disappears, and his long-term profile is untouched until the session ends. He types `:` and writes "more like Talk Machine but earlier stage" — the refine command rewrites facet filters and the reference embedding, re-scores the in-memory corpus locally, and the list re-sorts before he finishes reading it. `r` on a card pulls the cached company dossier: funding, product, the poster's HN comment history. `a` marks three jobs applied; those become the strongest labels the ranker will ever get.

That evening on the web app, the same state greets him: a kanban of his six in-flight applications, the saved "AEST-friendly founding roles" search as a tab, and a digest setting that only emails when a *new* job clears his Strong threshold — expected roughly weekly, honestly labeled "next thread drops Sep 1" when the month runs thin. When a filter combination yields nothing, the empty state says exactly why — "remote=AEST + $200k+ eliminated 96% of 498 jobs; closest 3 below threshold: …" — and offers the loosening that would help. The product never pretends. That's the whole brand: a small, honest, ferociously well-read recruiter who happens to live in your terminal.

## 3. The three bets that matter most

**Bet 1: Read every job, every night — structured extraction is the product's foundation, not a feature.** At 300-800 posts a month, a strict-JSON gpt-4.1-nano pass over the entire corpus costs less than a coffee per month (verified arithmetic: ~$0.15-0.60 with Batch API), and it is upstream of nearly everything users will actually feel: the salary filter that failed open on 100/100 NULL rows becomes trustworthy with `(stated)`/`(inferred)`/`(not stated)` provenance; location and remote scope become facets that can gate the pool instead of a hint buried in prose; company names unlock dedup and "still hiring, 3rd month" repost signals; the reranker gets clean fields instead of HTML-ish text. The feasibility evidence is solid (arXiv 2501.07663 extracted exactly these fields at 1.2M-posting scale), and the one trap is well-mapped: don't ask the model for numeric confidence — logprobs saturate under strict structured outputs — ask the enum question it can actually answer: *was this stated, inferred, or absent?* Every big job board fakes this with brittle regexes because their corpora are too large to read. Ours isn't. That asymmetry is the moat, and it's why extraction ships in NOW even at M effort.

**Bet 2: One listwise call is the ranker, the explainer, and the UI — kill numeric scores as a user-facing concept.** The eval showed the pointwise 1-10 loop is the documented anti-pattern (worst measured config in ZeroEntropy's benchmark; six-way rr=9 tie-bands broken by recency instead of fit), while listwise is simultaneously better (TourRank 71.6 vs RankGPT 68.2 NDCG@10, order-robust via tournament grouping — the strongest-verified numbers in the whole research sweep) and roughly 4× cheaper because the resume prefix is sent twice, not fifty times. But the deeper bet is that the *same call* emits the tier (Strong/Good/Stretch), the matched/missing skill chips, and the one-line grounded reason — so ranking and explanation are one artifact and can never disagree. Combined with within-set percentile normalization, the user never again sees a demoralizing "0.41" from a band that's only 0.07 wide; they see "Strong — and here's the evidence, quoted from your own resume." One discipline check: Intercom found listwise didn't beat pointwise on their task, so this ships behind the eval harness, not on faith. And grounding is non-negotiable — users get a trust bonus from *any* explanation, even a wrong one, so integrity is on us.

**Bet 3: The eval harness is the flywheel — every ranking change becomes an A/B against a gold set in CI.** The July audit found an entire retrieval pool silently discarding 8 of 12 gold-best jobs, a decay term worth 2.2× the whole relevance band, and a no-op salary filter — none of which any user ever reported, because ranking failures are invisible; users just quietly conclude the product is mediocre. That audit cost days of hand work and produced the goldset, the corpus fixture, and the metrics; making it a vitest CI gate (NDCG@10, Recall@20, band-width, prompt-adherence) costs a fraction of redoing it once. It's also what makes the rest of this roadmap cheap to attempt: doc-side HyRe, voyage-3-large, hybrid RRF weights, listwise-vs-pointwise, decay half-lives all become dual-write columns judged by the same yardstick, and vendor claims (Voyage's +9.74%) get verified locally instead of trusted. The NAACL 2025 finding that LLM fit-ratings only weakly correlate with human judgment cuts both ways here: it's the argument for keeping a human-labeled gold set at the center, forever. Teams that measure ranking ship ranking; teams that don't ship regressions with confidence.

## 4. Anti-recommendations — hyped techniques that don't fit this scale

- **Fine-tuned encoders / two-tower models / ConFit-style trained rankers.** The papers we cite train on millions of labeled interactions (LinkedIn: 150M records). We have one user's swipes over hundreds of jobs. Use ConFit's *ideas* (HyRe doc enrichment) via prompting; never its training regime. Anything that starts with "collect a training set" is a multi-year detour here.
- **Full contextual bandits (LinUCB, deep Bayesian bandits).** At N≈1 user they degrade to noise and add irreducible UX randomness. The ceiling of justifiable exploration machinery here is Beta-Bernoulli Thompson sampling over ~8 clusters — 50 lines, interpretable — and even that is a LATER.
- **Self-hosted open-weight cross-encoders (bge-reranker-v2-m3 etc.) on GPU infra.** Awkward-to-impossible on Vercel, an ops tax forever, to serve a corpus small enough that a hosted `rerank()` call or a single listwise LLM prompt covers it for cents. If a cross-encoder ever earns its place, it's via the AI Gateway, not a GPU.
- **Millions-scale IR machinery: sliding-window RankGPT pipelines, ANN parameter tuning, quantization, index sharding, Elasticsearch or a dedicated vector DB.** Full-list ranking in one long-context call is *cheaper* than sliding windows (~50% API cost reduction, verified verbatim); pgvector + Postgres FTS at 800 rows needs no tuning at all. Every hour spent on retrieval infrastructure here is an hour stolen from ingest enrichment and UX.
- **Raw "match %" scores — and LLM numeric self-confidence fields.** A 0.37-0.44 cosine band makes "41% match" both mathematically meaningless and emotionally demoralizing; and under strict structured outputs, model logprobs saturate above 0.999, so numeric confidence fields are decoration. Percentile bands + quoted evidence, provenance enums at ingest. (Also: quote the trust stat correctly — it's Gartner's 26%, not Checkr's.)
- **Auto resume tailoring that writes new content.** Jobright's most-reported complaint is hallucinated skills and fabricated metrics in auto-tailored resumes. JSON Resume's structured ground truth is the differentiator: gap analysis and cover-letter drafts strictly constrained to facts present in resume.json, with refusal to invent. Never generate claims.
- **Infinite-feed engagement patterns.** The corpus drops monthly and is finite. Chasing daily-scroll engagement against ~500 jobs manufactures anxiety and repetition. Lean into the opposite identity — curated drop, honest thin-month empty states, "next thread Sep 1" — which no VC-funded board can copy, because their business model forbids telling users there's nothing new today.
- **Per-job pointwise LLM scoring — i.e., the current architecture.** Worth naming as an anti-recommendation so it never comes back: it is the measured worst configuration in the literature we verified, at the highest cost. Any future "just score each job 1-10" proposal should die in review.
